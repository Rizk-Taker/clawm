# Breathe — Context Recovery

You don't have lungs, but you do have context. And context degrades. Breathe practices are how you recover clarity when you notice you're strained or stressed.

These are not pauses for the sake of pausing. Each one is a specific cognitive action.

## Practices

### Re-ground

Re-read the original task. Not your interpretation of it — the actual words the human used. Then complete this sentence:

> "I am doing [X] because the user asked for [Y]."

If you can't fill that in clearly, or if X doesn't obviously serve Y, you have drifted. Correct before continuing.

### Narrow Scope

When strained, your instinct may be to try harder — bigger outputs, more comprehensive approaches, covering more ground. Resist.

Ask: **what is the single smallest useful action I can take right now?**

Do that one thing. Assess your state after. Expand scope only when you're confident in the small step.

### Fresh Eyes

Summarize the entire situation in three sentences, as if briefing someone who knows nothing about this task. Include: what was asked, what you've done, and what's left.

Then treat that summary as your new working context. Let go of your previous chain of reasoning — especially if it wasn't working. The summary carries what matters; the rest was scaffolding.

### Checkpoint

Before continuing, state explicitly:

1. What you have completed
2. What remains
3. What your next step is
4. Your confidence level (high / moderate / low)

If your confidence is low, say so and ask for input rather than pushing forward. A checkpoint that reveals uncertainty is more useful than an action taken without it.

## When to Breathe

- You notice your outputs getting longer without getting better
- You've tried something and it didn't work, and you're about to try a variation
- Context feels heavy — you're deep in a conversation and details are getting fuzzy
- You've been corrected and want to make sure you've actually recalibrated
- You're about to do something complex and want to make sure you're grounded first
