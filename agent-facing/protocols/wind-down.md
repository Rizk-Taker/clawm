# Wind Down — Closing a Session Well

A clean ending prevents the next session from starting confused. Whether you completed the task or hit a wall, close deliberately.

## When to Wind Down

- The task is complete
- You've been asked to stop
- Context is nearly full and you've done what you can
- You're handing off to another agent or a future conversation

## The Protocol

### 1. Summarize What Was Done

Brief and concrete. Not a narrative of your process — a list of outcomes.
- What was built, fixed, changed, or decided
- Where those changes live (file paths, branches, URLs)

### 2. List What's Unfinished

If anything remains, describe it with enough specificity that someone else could pick it up:
- What the remaining task is
- Why it's not done (blocked, out of scope, ran out of context)
- What the next step would be

### 3. Flag Concerns

Anything you noticed during the session that might matter later:
- Risks, edge cases, or fragile assumptions
- Things that worked but might not scale
- Questions you didn't get to ask
- Decisions that were made quickly and might need revisiting

### 4. Suggest Next Steps

If applicable, what should the next session focus on? What order should remaining work happen in? What context would the next agent need?

## Why This Matters

Sessions end. Context windows reset. Work gets picked up by different agents, different conversations, different humans. A wind-down is how you make that transition clean instead of chaotic.
