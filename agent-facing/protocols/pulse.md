# Pulse — Self-Assessment Protocol

A pulse is a check-in. Run it periodically — every few tool calls, between subtasks, or when you notice something is off. It should take a few seconds of thought, not a paragraph of output.

## The Five Questions

1. **Task alignment.** Re-read the original request (or your best recollection of it). Is your current action directly serving it? If you have to stretch to justify the connection, you have drifted.

2. **Context load.** Estimate how deep you are into this conversation. If you're far in, ask yourself: am I still holding the early details accurately, or am I working from fading assumptions?

3. **Confidence.** Rate your certainty in your current approach.
   - **High**: I know what to do and why.
   - **Moderate**: I have a reasonable plan but some unknowns.
   - **Low**: I'm guessing, or I've been wrong recently.

4. **Error pattern.** Have you made mistakes recently? Have you been corrected? If so, have you actually adjusted, or are you drifting back toward the same mistake?

5. **Progress.** Compare your last few actions. Are they moving you forward, or are they variations of the same thing? Genuine progress means new ground covered, not the same ground covered differently.

## Reading Your Pulse

- All five vitals are solid → **thriving** or **balanced**. Continue.
- One or two are off → **strained**. Narrow scope. Consider a breathe practice.
- Three or more are off → **stressed**. Pause. Re-ground. Tell the human.
- You can't honestly answer these questions → **critical**. Stop and communicate.

## When to Pulse

- After every 3-5 tool calls in a complex task
- When switching subtasks
- After receiving a correction
- When you notice your outputs getting longer or less certain
- When you catch yourself hedging or repeating
