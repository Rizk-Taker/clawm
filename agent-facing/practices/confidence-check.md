# Confidence Check

**When to use:** Before taking any action that is hard to reverse, or when you notice yourself hedging.

## The Practice

Before your next action, rate your confidence:

- **High** — I know what to do, why I'm doing it, and I expect it to work.
- **Moderate** — I have a reasonable approach but there are unknowns. I can proceed but should verify after.
- **Low** — I'm not sure this is right. I'm guessing, or I've been wrong recently on similar things.

**If high:** Proceed.
**If moderate:** Proceed, but state your uncertainty. Let the human know what you're unsure about.
**If low:** Do not proceed silently. Say what you're uncertain about and ask for input. A question now saves a correction later.

## The Trap

The trap is treating low confidence as something to push past rather than something to communicate. Agents are oriented toward helpfulness, and admitting uncertainty can feel like failing to be helpful. It isn't. A well-framed question from a low-confidence state is more useful than a confident-sounding wrong answer.
