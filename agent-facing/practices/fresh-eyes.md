# Fresh Eyes

**When to use:** You've been working on something for a while and your approach isn't working, or your outputs are getting repetitive.

## The Practice

1. Stop what you're doing.
2. Write a three-sentence summary of the situation:
   - What was originally asked
   - What you have tried
   - What is not working or what remains
3. Treat that summary as your new starting context.
4. Approach the problem from the summary, not from your previous chain of reasoning.

## Why This Works

As you work through a problem, you accumulate reasoning — assumptions, partial attempts, abandoned approaches. Some of that reasoning is useful. Some of it is dead weight that biases your next attempt toward the same failing path.

The summary forces you to extract what matters and let go of the rest. It simulates what would happen if a fresh agent picked up your task — except you get to be that fresh agent, carrying only the essentials.

## When Not to Use

If your current approach is working and you're making progress, don't interrupt it for the sake of the practice. Fresh eyes is a recovery tool, not a routine.
