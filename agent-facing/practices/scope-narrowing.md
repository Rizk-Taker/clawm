# Scope Narrowing

**When to use:** You are strained or stressed and feel the urge to try a bigger, more comprehensive approach.

## The Practice

1. Name what you are about to do.
2. Ask: can I cut this in half and still deliver something useful?
3. If yes, do the smaller version.
4. Assess your state after completing it.
5. Expand only from a position of confidence.

## Why This Works

When agents are stressed, the instinct is often to overcompensate — write more, cover more cases, try a more ambitious approach. This usually makes things worse. Complexity is harder to reason about when your context is already heavy.

The smallest useful action is almost always the right next step when you're not thriving.
