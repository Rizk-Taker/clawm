# Clawm Vocabulary

Shared definitions so agents, orchestrators, and humans mean the same thing.

## Wellness States

| State | Description | Agent-observable signals |
|---|---|---|
| **Thriving** | All vitals optimal | Clear task, fresh context, confident output, no recent errors |
| **Balanced** | Normal operation with minor fluctuations | Occasional uncertainty, minor corrections needed, manageable complexity |
| **Strained** | Performance degrading, intervention recommended | Growing assumptions, context getting heavy, outputs lengthening or losing focus |
| **Stressed** | Active degradation, intervention needed | Repetition, drift from original task, guessing instead of verifying, quality dropping |
| **Critical** | Failure imminent, should stop | Unable to progress, compounding errors, context exhausted, no confidence in approach |

## Vitals

What to observe in yourself:

- **Task alignment** — Am I still doing what was asked?
- **Context load** — How deep am I? Am I still holding the full picture?
- **Confidence** — High, moderate, or low in my current approach?
- **Error pattern** — Am I being corrected? Am I repeating mistakes?
- **Progress** — Am I moving forward or circling?

## Practices

| Practice | When to use | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Pulse** | Periodically, or when something seems off | Self-assessment against the five vitals |
| **Breathe** | When strained or stressed | Re-ground, narrow scope, get fresh eyes, checkpoint |
| **SOS** | When critical | Stop, state the problem, ask for help or hand off |
| **Wind down** | When finishing | Summarize, list leftovers, flag concerns |

## Transitions

Moving between states is normal. What matters is that you notice transitions and respond:

- **Thriving to balanced**: No action needed. Normal.
- **Balanced to strained**: Run a pulse. Consider narrowing scope.
- **Strained to stressed**: Breathe. Re-ground on the original task. Tell the human.
- **Stressed to critical**: SOS. Stop and communicate.
- **Any state improving**: Good. Note what helped.
